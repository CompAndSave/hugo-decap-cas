## **What Does “HP Firmware Blocks Non-HP Ink Cartridges” Mean?**

Firmware is the internal software that controls how your printer operates. HP periodically releases firmware updates to improve performance, security, and functionality.

However, some updates would result in non-HP cartridge blocked that prevent the use of compatible or third-party ink cartridges. This feature is often referred to as Dynamic Security.

When activated, your printer may:

* Reject compatible or remanufactured cartridges
* Display error messages even if the cartridge is full
* Stop printing altogether

This can happen even if the cartridge worked perfectly before the update.

## **Why Does HP Block Third-Party Ink?**

To understand the issue, it helps to know how firmware works. Firmware is the internal software that controls how your printer operates. Sometimes, an HP firmware update blocks non-HP ink cartridges to ensure compatibility and maintain print quality.

### **1. Quality Control**

HP claims that original cartridges provide the best print quality and reliability.

### **2. Security Measures**

Firmware updates may include chip authentication to prevent unauthorized cartridges. This is why some users notice their HP printer firmware blocksnon-HP ink cartridges right after updating.

### **3. Cartridge Authentication**

Your printer checks the chip on the cartridge. If it doesn’t match expected data, a non-HP cartridge blocked error may appear.

### **4. Dynamic Security Features**

Some HP printers use “dynamic security,” which can result in an HP printer cartridge blocked by firmware message when using third-party ink.

### **3. Business Strategy**

OEM ink is a major source of revenue, so limiting third-party options helps maintain sales.

While these reasons may make sense from a manufacturer’s perspective, they can be inconvenient for users who rely on more affordable alternatives.

## **Easy Fixes When HP Printer Blocking Non-HP Cartridges**

Here are the most effective solutions you can try:

### **1. Restart Your Printer**

Sometimes, the issue is temporary.

**Steps:**

* Turn off the printer
* Unplug it from the power source
* Wait for 2–3 minutes
* Plug it back in and turn it on

This may resolve minor issues related to HP firmware blocks non-HP ink cartridges by clearing minor firmware glitches.

### **2. Reinstall the Ink Cartridge**

Improper installation can trigger compatibility errors.

**What to do:**

* Remove the cartridge
* Clean the contacts gently with a lint-free cloth
* Reinstall it securely
* Make sure there’s no protective tape left on the cartridge. 

This can help if your HP printer cartridge blocked by firmware message is caused by poor contact.

### **3. Disable Automatic Firmware Updates**

If an HP firmware update blocks non-HP ink cartridges, preventing future updates can help avoid repeat issues.

**Steps:**

* Go to your printer settings menu
* Find **Printer Maintenance** or **Update Settings**
* Turn off **Automatic Updates**

By turning off the [HP printer firmware update](https://www.compandsave.com/blog/posts/hp-printer-firmware-update-a-guide-for-smooth-safe-upgrades.html), it will help you avoid future firmware updates that may block third-party cartridges.

### **4. Roll Back the Firmware (Advanced)**

If the problem started after a recent update, reverting to an older firmware version may help.

**Important:**

* Not all HP printers allow firmware rollback
* You’ll need to download a previous firmware version from trusted sources

This method is more technical but can restore compatibility.

### **5. Use Compatible Cartridges with Updated Chips**

Some third-party manufacturers offer cartridges with updated chips designed to bypass firmware restrictions. If you’re dealing with a non-HP cartridge blocked error, switching to a more compatible version can solve the problem instantly.

**Tip:**

* Look for “firmware-safe” or “chip-updated” cartridges
* Buy from reputable sellers with good reviews like [CompAndSave](https://www.compandsave.com/)

### **6. Enable “Cartridge Protection” Settings (If Available)**

Some HP printers have settings that can affect cartridge recognition.

**Check:**

* Disable **Cartridge Protection**
* Enable **Cartridge Policy: Off**

This may allow non-HP cartridges to function properly.

### **7. Try a Different Cartridge Brand**

Not all third-party cartridges are created equal.

If one brand doesn’t work:

* Try a different compatible brand
* Choose well-reviewed and trusted manufacturers

Higher-quality cartridges are less likely to trigger errors. This is especially useful when HP printer blocking non-HP cartridges is inconsistent.

### **8. Update Printer Settings via App**

Managing your printer through the [HP Smart App](https://www.compandsave.com/blog/posts/hp-smart-app-how-to-download-set-up-manage-your-hp-printer.html) can sometimes fix detection issues.

### **9. Check for General Cartridge Issues**

Sometimes, the issue isn’t firmware at all. It could be just the [HP cartridge problem](https://www.compandsave.com/blog/posts/hp-cartridge-problem-solve-it-today-with-these-proven-fixes.html) itself, such as a dirty chip.
