## **Why You Should Avoid HP+ Restrictions**

When setting up your HP printer, you may be prompted to activate **HP+ or Instant Ink**. While it seems convenient, **opting in locks your printer into using only original HP cartridges.** That means you can’t use affordable alternatives or remanufactured ink, even if they’re fully compatible.

If saving money and having flexibility are your priorities, simply skip HP+ during setup. This way, you stay free to choose high-quality, low-cost cartridges that give you the same great prints for less.

## **Frequently Asked Questions**

### **1. How do I know if an ink cartridge will work with my printer?**

The safest way is to check your printer’s model number and match it with the compatible printer list provided by the seller. If you’re unsure, most reputable stores (like CompAndSave) let you confirm compatibility through customer support or by searching your model directly on their website. Taking a minute to confirm prevents wasted money and frustration later.

### **2. What should I do if my printer doesn’t recognize the cartridge?**

You can remove and reinstall the cartridge, ensuring it clicks firmly in place. If the issue continues, gently clean the cartridge contacts with a lint-free cloth. Some HP printers release firmware updates that block third-party ink, so [disabling automatic updates](https://www.compandsave.com/blog/posts/how-to-disable-auto-hp-printer-firmware-updates-hp-63-65.html) in your printer settings can help keep your cartridges functioning smoothly.

### **3. Is there a difference between standard-yield and high-yield cartridges?**

Yes, and it often affects your printing costs. Standard-yield cartridges are suitable for light users, but if you print regularly, high-yield cartridges almost always provide a better value. They cost a bit more upfront, but produce more pages, which lowers your cost per page and reduces the frequency of replacements.
