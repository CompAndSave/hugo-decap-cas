## **What Is Duplex Scanning and How Does It Work?**

Duplex scanning refers to the ability of a scanner to capture both sides of a page in one pass. Instead of manually flipping each sheet, the printer’s laser printer with double sided scanner feature automatically reads both sides, creating digital copies quickly. This is especially useful for contracts, reports, or invoices.

### **How an All-in-One Laser Printer with Duplex Scanning Processes Double-Sided Documents**

While both involve double-sided document handling, duplex scanning and duplex printing are different. Duplex scanning captures images from both sides of a paper, while duplex printing uses a laser printer with duplex printing and scanning to print on both sides automatically. Many modern all-in-one laser printers with duplex scanning combine these features, offering seamless two-sided printing and scanning in one device.

### **Single-Pass vs Double-Pass Duplex Scanning (Critical Difference)**

| Feature | Single-Pass Duplex Scanning | Double-Pass Duplex Scanning |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Scanning Method | Both sides scanned simultaneously | One side scanned, then flipped for the other |
| Speed | Very fast; ideal for high-volume jobs | Slower; doubles scan time per page |
| Accuracy | High; minimal misfeeds | Moderate; risk of misalignment |
| Wear & Maintenance | Low; less stress on feeder | Higher; more mechanical wear |
| Best Use | Frequent scanning, offices, laser printers with duplex scanning | Occasional scanning, low-volume users |

Duplex scanning and duplex printing serve different purposes. A laser printer with duplex scanning scans both sides automatically, while duplex printing outputs double-sided documents. Many users confuse the two, so verify your needs before purchasing.

Not all models include duplex scanning. Only all-in-one laser printers with duplex scanning offer automatic double-sided scanning, while some provide just duplex printing. Look specifically for a laser printer with double sided scanner for efficient document workflows.
