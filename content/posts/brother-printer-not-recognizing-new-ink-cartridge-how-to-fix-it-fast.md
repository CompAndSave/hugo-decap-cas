Replacing an ink cartridge should be simple—but it quickly becomes frustrating when your [Brother printer cannot detect cartridge](https://www.compandsave.com/blog/posts/brother-printer-cannot-detect-cartridge-real-fixes-not-fluff.html) and refuses to recognize the new one.

If your Brother printer not recognizing new ink cartridge problem suddenly appears, don’t panic. This is a common issue experienced by many Brother printer users, whether using genuine, compatible, or remanufactured cartridges.

There may be instances that your Brother printer will not recognize new ink cartridge, or notice that the printer simply won’t print after installation. The good news? In many cases, the problem can be fixed at home with a few quick troubleshooting steps.

This guide will walk you through practical and beginner-friendly solutions to help get your Brother printer working again fast.

## **Why Is My Brother Printer Will Not Recognize New Ink Cartridge?**

There are several reasons why a Brother printer may stop recognizing a newly installed cartridge.

**Common causes include:**

* Incorrect cartridge installation
* Dirty cartridge contacts
* Protective packaging left on the cartridge
* Cartridge compatibility problems
* Firmware-related issues
* Temporary printer glitches

Sometimes, even a brand-new cartridge may fail to register properly due to a small setup mistake.

Before assuming the cartridge is defective, it’s worth trying a few simple fixes first.

## **How to Fix Brother Printer Not Recognizing New Ink Cartridge?**

### **Step 1: Remove and Reinstall the Ink Cartridge**

One of the easiest fixes is reinstalling the cartridge properly.

If your Brother printer will not recognize new ink cartridge, start by removing and reinserting the cartridge to ensure everything is aligned correctly.

**Here’s how:**

1. Turn on your printer
2. Open the cartridge access cover
3. Remove the new cartridge carefully
4. Check for any plastic tabs or protective tape still attached
5. Reinsert the cartridge firmly into the correct color slot
6. Close the cover and allow the printer to detect the cartridge

Sometimes the cartridge clicks into place incorrectly, which prevents the printer from recognizing it.

After reinstalling, wait a few moments to see if the issue resolves itself.

### **Step 2: Check for Protective Tape or Packaging**

This may sound obvious, but it happens more often than people realize.

**New cartridges often include:**

* Plastic protective clips
* Sealing tape
* Orange protective covers

If any of these remain attached, the printer may fail to read the cartridge.

Many cases of a Brother printer not recognizing new ink cartridge issue are solved simply by removing leftover packaging materials.

Double-check the cartridge before moving on to more advanced troubleshooting.

### **Step 3: Clean the Cartridge Contacts**

Dirty or blocked contacts can interrupt communication between the cartridge and printer.

If your Brother printer won’t recognize new ink cartridge, cleaning the contacts may help.

**What you’ll need:**

* A lint-free cloth
* Cotton swab (optional)
* Small amount of rubbing alcohol or distilled water

**Steps:**

1. Remove the cartridge
2. Gently clean the metal contacts on the cartridge
3. Wipe away dust or dried ink residue
4. Let everything dry completely
5. Reinstall the cartridge

Be gentle during cleaning to avoid damaging delicate components.

This step often resolves cartridge detection errors without requiring replacement.

## **What If the Brother Printer Will Not Recognize New Ink Cartridge After Installation?**

It can feel confusing when a brand-new cartridge still doesn’t work.

If your Brother printer does not recognize new ink cartridge after installation, here are a few possible explanations:

**Wrong cartridge model**

Even if cartridges look similar, using the incorrect cartridge number can cause compatibility problems.

**Defective cartridge**

Sometimes new cartridges are faulty out of the box.

**Damaged chip or sensor**

A damaged cartridge chip may stop communication with the printer.

**Installation error**

If the cartridge is not fully locked into place, the printer may fail to detect it.

Checking these possibilities can save time and frustration. Here’s what you can do:

### **1. Restart or Reset the Printer**

Temporary software glitches can sometimes prevent cartridge recognition.

A quick way to troubleshoot this [Brother printer issue](https://www.compandsave.com/blog/posts/brother-printer-issue-is-ink-lockdown-making-them-the-next-hp.html) is to restart the printer completely.

**To reset a Brother printer:**

1. Turn off the printer
2. Unplug the power cable
3. Wait for about 60 seconds
4. Plug the printer back in
5. Turn it on again

In some cases, restarting helps refresh cartridge communication and removes temporary errors.

If needed, you may also search your printer model instructions to properly [reset a Brother printer](https://www.compandsave.com/blog/posts/how-to-reset-a-brother-printer-step-by-step-guide.html) through the settings menu.

### **2. Check for Firmware-Related Issues**

Printer firmware sometimes affects cartridge recognition.

Some users report situations where a [Brother printer firmware update affects non-OEM cartridges](https://www.compandsave.com/blog/posts/how-brother-printer-firmware-update-affects-non-oem-cartridges.html), particularly with compatible or remanufactured ink.

Firmware is built-in software that helps control printer behavior. Occasionally, updates may change how the printer verifies cartridges.

This does not happen to every printer, but if your issue started shortly after an update, it may be worth checking.

Before assuming firmware is the cause, try the easier troubleshooting steps first.

A brand-neutral approach is to confirm cartridge compatibility and installation before considering software-related fixes.

### **3. Try Another Cartridge**

If none of the earlier solutions work to address the Brother printer not recognizing new ink cartridge, then the cartridge itself may be defective.

**Try:**

* Installing another new cartridge
* Using a genuine cartridge for testing
* Testing a compatible cartridge from a trusted supplier

If the second cartridge works normally, the first cartridge may simply be faulty.

This is one of the quickest ways to isolate the problem.

### **4. Update or Reinstall Printer Drivers**

Sometimes the issue isn’t the cartridge at all.

Older drivers or software glitches can interfere with printer performance.

Visit Brother’s support page and:

* Check for updated printer drivers
* Install available updates
* Restart your computer and printer

This step may help if your printer still refuses to detect cartridges.

## **What If My Brother Printer Won’t Recognize New Ink Cartridge?**

If you’re still thinking, “my Brother printer will not recognize new ink cartridge,” don’t worry—there are still a few things to check.

Ask yourself:

* Is the cartridge installed correctly?
* Did I remove all the protective tape?
* Is the cartridge compatible with my printer model?
* Did the issue begin after a firmware update?
* Could the cartridge be defective?

Most recognition issues can be solved through simple troubleshooting rather than expensive repairs.

## **How to Prevent Cartridge Recognition Problems**

While not every issue can be avoided, these habits may reduce future problems:

### **Handle cartridges carefully**

Avoid touching chips or contacts directly.

### **Install cartridges properly**

Listen for the click during installation.

### **Store cartridges correctly**

Avoid extreme heat or humidity.

### **Buy reliable cartridges**

Whether genuine or compatible, quality matters.

### **Avoid unnecessary firmware changes**

Review updates before installing when possible.

Following these simple habits may help reduce future cartridge errors.

Whether your Brother printer will not recognize new ink cartridge because of installation issues, dirty contacts, compatibility concerns, or software glitches, starting with the basics often saves time and money.

If your printer still refuses to cooperate, don’t give up too quickly. A careful check of the cartridge, printer settings, and firmware may be all it takes to get back to printing again.
