Your Canon printer was working perfectly yesterday, but today it suddenly shows “offline” or refuses to connect. Situations like this can be frustrating, especially when you don’t know where the problem is coming from.

In many cases, the issue is not the printer itself but the network connection behind it. More specifically, it often comes down to the [IP address changing](https://www.compandsave.com/blog/posts/how-to-set-or-change-ip-address-on-printer-windows-mac.html) or not being recognized by your device.

If you are trying to find Canon printer IP address, this guide will walk you through every method in a clear and practical way. At the same time, you will also understand how the IP address on a Canon printer works, so you can prevent these issues in the future instead of just fixing them once.

## **What Is a Canon Printer IP Address?**

A Canon printer IP address is a unique set of numbers assigned to your printer when it connects to a network. This address allows your computer, phone, or tablet to locate the printer and send print jobs to it.

You will usually see it in a format like: 192.168.1.25

To make this easier to understand, think of your home network as a neighborhood. Every device connected to it has its own address, and your printer is no different. Without the correct printer IP address in Canon, your device simply cannot find where to send the document.

This is why even a perfectly working printer can appear “offline” if the IP address changes or is entered incorrectly.

## **Why You Need to Find Your Canon Printer IP Address**

There are several real-world situations where knowing how to find Canon printer IP address becomes essential. Most users only realize this after running into a problem, but understanding it ahead of time can save a lot of frustration.

Here are the most common scenarios:

### **1. Your Printer Keeps Going Offline**

This usually happens when your computer is still trying to connect to an old IP address. Since the printer now has a new one, the connection fails even though the printer is turned on and working.

### **2. You Are Setting Up a New Device**

When connecting your printer to a new laptop or phone, you may need to manually enter the IP address. This ensures the device connects directly to the correct printer without confusion.

### **3. Your Network Has Changed**

If you recently changed or reset your Wi-Fi router, the network settings will also change. As a result, your printer may be assigned a completely different IP address.

### **4. You Want a Stable Printing Setup**

If you rely on your printer regularly, knowing its IP address lets you set a static IP address. This prevents the address from changing and avoids repeated connection issues.

All of these situations highlight the same thing: once you understand your printer’s IP, you gain more control over how your printer behaves on your network.

## **Method 1: Find Canon Printer IP Address from the Printer Screen**

If your Canon printer has a built-in display, this is the fastest and most straightforward method. You can access the network information directly without needing a computer.

**Step-by-step:**

1. Turn on your printer
2. Tap Menu or Settings
3. Go to Network Settings
4. Select Wireless LAN Settings
5. Tap Confirm Network Settings
6. Locate the IP address

The number displayed there is your Canon IP address printer, and it is the exact value your devices use to communicate with the printer.

This method is especially useful because it works even when your printer is not currently connected to a computer. It gives you direct access to the most accurate network information available.

## **Method 2: Print a Network Configuration Page**

For printers without a screen, printing a network configuration page is one of the most reliable alternatives. This page includes all the necessary network details, including the IP address.

**Steps:**

1. Make sure the printer is powered on
2. Press and hold the Stop/Resume button
3. Wait for the printer to print a configuration page
4. Find the IP address in the network section

This method is often recommended because it provides a physical copy of your printer’s settings. It is a simple yet [effective way to find IP address](https://www.compandsave.com/blog/posts/how-to-find-printer-ip-address-step-by-step-guide.html) for Canon printer, especially when other methods are not accessible.

## **Method 3: Find Canon Printer IP Address Using Windows**

If your printer is already connected to a Windows computer, you can locate the IP address through system settings without touching the printer itself.

**Steps:**

1. Open Control Panel
2. Go to Devices and Printers
3. Right-click your Canon printer
4. Select Printer Properties
5. Click on the Ports tab

You will see a port labeled something like: Standard TCP/IP Port – 192.168.1.25

This number is the IP address for a Canon printer, and it represents the active connection your computer is using.

This method is convenient because it lets you confirm the IP address your system is currently using. If there is a mismatch, you will immediately see it here.

## **Method 4: Find Canon Printer IP Address on Mac**

Mac users can also easily locate the printer’s IP address through system settings. The process is slightly different but just as simple.

**Steps:**

1. Open System Settings
2. Navigate to Printers & Scanners
3. Select your Canon printer
4. Click Options & Supplies
5. Check the General tab

The IP address will be displayed within the printer information. This method is particularly helpful when troubleshooting wireless printing issues, as it confirms whether your Mac is connected to the correct printer on the network.

## **Method 5: Find the IP Address Through Your Router**

Your router acts as the central hub of your network, which means it keeps a record of every connected device. This includes your Canon printer.

**Steps:**

1. Open a browser and enter your router’s IP (e.g., 192.168.1.1)
2. Log in using your credentials
3. Look for Connected Devices or DHCP Client List
4. Locate your Canon printer
5. Note the IP address assigned to it

This method for finding the IP address on a Canon printer is extremely useful when your printer is not appearing on your computer. It allows you to verify that the printer is connected to the network and to identify the correct IP address directly from the source.

## **Understanding Dynamic vs Static IP Addresses**

One of the most common reasons users struggle with printer connections is the difference between dynamic and static IP addresses.

A dynamic IP address is automatically assigned by your router and can change over time. This is the default setup for most home networks, and while it works well in many cases, it can cause connection issues when the address changes unexpectedly.

A static IP address, on the other hand, is manually assigned and remains the same. This creates a more stable connection because your devices always know exactly where the printer is.

For users who print frequently or share a printer across multiple devices, setting a static IP is a simple upgrade that can prevent recurring problems.

## **Frequently Asked Questions**

### **How do I find my Canon printer IP address if it’s not showing on my computer?**

If your printer does not appear on your computer, you can check the printer screen or print a network configuration page. Another reliable option is to log in to your router to view connected devices. These methods allow you to locate the printer's IP address even when your computer cannot detect it.

### **Does every Canon printer have an IP address?**

Yes, any Canon printer connected to a network will have an IP address assigned to it. This applies to both wired and wireless connections. Without an IP address, devices would not be able to communicate with the printer.

### **Why does my Canon printer IP address keep changing?**

This usually happens because your router assigns dynamic IP addresses by default. Each time the network resets, the printer may receive a new address. Setting a static IP can prevent this from happening.
